Project Overview

In this project, I have highlited the various stages in the "Data Analysis Process" along with the importance of each stage and discovered some of the most interesting insights through data exploration.

How I wrangled, analyzed and visualized Twitter user account ‘WeRateDogs’ that rates people's dogs with a humorous comment about the dog.

My analysis of bike-sharing system data to derive insights for better business decisions.

Key Insights

  • The dogs were categorized into four stages (pupper, doggo, puppo, floofer). Among these dog stages, the largest number of occurrences was the pupper stage, followed by doggo and puppo.
  • Among the values for the rating numerators, 12 appears to be the most used value. It appeared 450 times. Followed by 10, 11, and 13 respectively.
  • There is a positive correlation (0.9129) between favorite count and retweet count. This shows that there is strong likelihood that, anyone who favorite a tweet is also going to retweet it.
  • A larger portion of the dogs in the data did not have names. Charlie happens to have the highest number of occurrences (11 times). Cooper, Oliver, Tucker, Penny and Lucy followed with a number of occurrences of 10 times each.

    What I learned

  • Handling real-world data is messy: issues like wrong data types, null values, inconsistent naming, and joined tables must be addressed.
  • It’s important to detect and fix quality issues (datatype problems, invalid values, missing data) and tidiness issues (e.g. combining related columns, merging tables).
  • Practiced communicating results in both technical and business-friendly ways.
  • Exploratory analysis and visualization help uncover patterns and relationships in data (e.g. correlations, frequencies, distributions).
  • Data cleaning, transformation, and merging are foundational steps before analysis.
